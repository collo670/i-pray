The Seven Sacraments of the Catholic Church

The sacraments are efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church, by which divine life is dispensed to us.

Baptism

Scripture: "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit" (Matthew 28:19)

Effect: Removes original sin and all personal sin, imparts sanctifying grace, makes us children of God, and incorporates us into the Church.

Minister: Ordinarily a bishop, priest, or deacon. In emergency, any person with the right intention can baptize.


Confirmation

Scripture: "And when Paul had laid his hands on them, the Holy Spirit came on them" (Acts 19:6)

Effect: Completes baptismal grace, gives special strength of the Holy Spirit, binds us more perfectly to the Church, and strengthens us to be witnesses of Christ.

Minister: Ordinarily the bishop. In special cases, a priest may administer it.


Eucharist

Scripture: "This is my body... This is my blood of the covenant, which will be shed for many" (Mark 14:22-24)

Effect: Increases our union with Christ and the Church, preserves and renews the life of grace, separates us from sin, and commits us to the poor.

Minister: A validly ordained priest (bishop or presbyter).


Penance (Reconciliation)

Scripture: "Receive the Holy Spirit. Whose sins you forgive are forgiven them, and whose sins you retain are retained" (John 20:22-23)

Effect: Reconciles us with God and the Church, restores sanctifying grace, remits eternal punishment, and provides peace and serenity of conscience.

Minister: A priest with the faculty to hear confessions.


Anointing of the Sick

Scripture: "Is anyone among you sick? He should summon the presbyters of the church, and they should pray over him and anoint him with oil in the name of the Lord" (James 5:14)

Effect: Unites the sick person to Christ's Passion, provides strength and peace, may bring about physical healing, and prepares for passing to eternal life.

Minister: A priest or bishop.


Holy Orders

Scripture: "Do not neglect the gift you have, which was conferred on you through the prophetic word with the imposition of hands of the presbyterate" (1 Timothy 4:14)

Effect: Imprints an indelible spiritual character, configures the ordained to Christ, and gives grace to exercise sacred ministry.

Minister: A bishop.


Matrimony

Scripture: "For this reason a man shall leave his father and mother and be joined to his wife, and the two shall become one flesh" (Ephesians 5:31)

Effect: Establishes a perpetual and exclusive bond between husband and wife, provides grace for married life, and sanctifies their love.

Minister: The spouses minister the sacrament to each other through their mutual consent.

"The sacraments are efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church, by which divine life is dispensed to us. The visible rites by which the sacraments are celebrated signify and make present the graces proper to each sacrament. They bear fruit in those who receive them with the required dispositions." - Catechism of the Catholic Church, 1131
