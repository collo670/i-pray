Daily Readings

Saturday, August 2, 2025 • Ordinary Time

First Reading

Leviticus 25:1, 8–17

The Lord spoke to Moses on Mount Sinai:

“You shall count seven weeks of years—seven times seven years—so that the period of seven weeks of years gives forty-nine years.

Then you shall sound the trumpet on the tenth day of the seventh month; on the Day of Atonement you shall sound the trumpet throughout all your land.

And you shall sanctify the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout the land to all its inhabitants. It shall be a jubilee for you: each of you shall return to his property, and each of you shall return to his clan.

For it is a jubilee; it shall be holy to you. You shall eat what the field yields of itself; you shall not sow, nor reap what grows of itself, nor gather the grapes from the undressed vines.

For the jubilee is holy; you may eat only the produce of the field.

In this year of jubilee, every man shall return to his possession.

When you make a sale to your neighbor or buy from your neighbor, you shall not wrong one another. You shall buy from your neighbor according to the number of years since the last jubilee; and he shall sell to you according to the number of years for crops.

The more years, the higher the price; the fewer years, the lower the price. For it is the number of harvests that he sells to you.

Do not oppress one another, but fear your God; for I am the Lord your God.”

Responsorial Psalm

Psalm 67:2–3, 5, 7–8

R. O God, let all the nations praise you!

May God have mercy on us and bless us; may he let his face shine upon us, that your way may be known upon earth, your saving power among all nations.

Let the peoples praise you, O God; let all the peoples praise you!

Let the nations be glad and exult; for you rule the peoples with justice and guide the nations upon earth.

R. O God, let all the nations praise you!

The earth has yielded its fruit; may God, our God, bless us. May God bless us, and may all the ends of the earth fear him!

R. O God, let all the nations praise you!

Gospel

Matthew 14:1–12

At that time, Herod the tetrarch heard about the fame of Jesus.

And he said to his servants, “This is John the Baptist. He has been raised from the dead; that is why mighty powers are at work in him.”

Now Herod had arrested John, bound him, and put him in prison because of Herodias, the wife of his brother Philip.

For John had been telling him, “It is not lawful for you to have her.”

Although Herod wanted to put him to death, he feared the crowd, because they regarded John as a prophet.

But on Herod’s birthday, the daughter of Herodias danced before the company and pleased Herod so much that he promised with an oath to give her whatever she might ask.

Prompted by her mother, she said, “Give me here on a platter the head of John the Baptist.”

The king was distressed, but because of his oaths and the guests, he ordered it to be given.

He had John beheaded in the prison, and his head was brought on a platter and given to the girl, who took it to her mother.

John’s disciples came and took the body and buried it; then they went and told Jesus.
